About Us

The elderly should not be seen as a depreciating asset -- with a proper set of policies in place,they could continue to lead rich and useful lives and contribute positively to the community. For every nation in the new economy of knowledge and human capital, the elderly were a valuable asset that deserved generation equity. For a country like India having the second highest population it becomes a dire need to look after its Human Resources if it has to become the next Superpower. We are a presently having the largest population of youth and that is the bedrock of our future. However, we are soon also going to have perhaps the largest population of the elderly and The elderly, as per the information gathered from Department of Geriatrics, AIIMS, tend to face gradual degradation of mental faculties which could lead to several disorders like Amnesia, Alzheimer disease, among others.

Globally, there is rise in the population of the elderly people with the generation of baby boomers having entered the age of 60 years . Old age is commonly associated with disorders such as high blood pressure and heart diseases. Along with this problem, it also leads to slowing of the brain functions. Memory lapse is the most common brain dysfunction that people notice and are aware of.

Globally, India houses the second most number of individuals suffering from dementia with an estimated 4.1 million people suffering from it as per the ‘Dementia India’ report published by the Alzheimer’s and Related Disorders Society of India. This is expected to double by 2035.
